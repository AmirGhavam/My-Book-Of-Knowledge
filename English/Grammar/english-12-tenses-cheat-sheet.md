# THE ULTIMATE 12-TENSE CHEAT SHEET 

## 1. Present Simple
### Uses + Examples
1) Habits & routines
    * I wake up at 7 every day.
    * She drinks coffee before work.
2) General facts & truths
    * Water boils at 100°C.
    * The Earth orbits the sun.
3) Permanent situations
    * He lives in Dubai.
    * They work at a hospital.
4) Schedules & timetables
    * The train leaves at 8 p.m.
    * The meeting starts at noon.
5) Stative verbs
    * I believe you.
    * She knows the answer.
6) Commentary / headlines
    * Messi scores in the 92nd minute!
    * Government raises taxes.

### Exceptions / Details
* Stative verbs can't be used in continuous: know, believe, want, seem…
* With frequency adverbs, order is: Subject + adverb + verb→ She always forgets her keys.

## 2. Present Continuous 

### Uses + Examples
1) Actions happening right now
    * She is typing the report.
    * They are eating dinner.
2) Temporary actions
    * I’m staying at my cousin’s place this week.
    * He’s working from home today.
3) Changing or developing situations
    * Prices are rising rapidly.
    * The climate is getting warmer.
4) Future arrangements
    * We’re meeting the client tomorrow.
    * I’m flying to London next week.
5) Annoying repeated actions (with always)
    * He is always losing his phone.
    * She is always interrupting me.

### Exceptions / Details
* Some stative verbs CAN be used in continuous with different meanings:
    * I’m having lunch. (eating, not “possessing”)
    * I’m seeing a doctor tomorrow. (appointment → not eyesight)

## 3. Present Perfect

### Uses + Examples
1) Life experiences (time not mentioned)
    * I have visited Japan twice.
    * She has tried sushi before.
2) Recent actions with present results
    * I’ve lost my keys. (I still don’t have them)
    * She has broken her arm. (her arm is still broken)
3) Unfinished time periods
    * I have read three books this week.
    * We’ve had many meetings today.
4) Changes over time
    * The city has grown a lot.
    * Your English has improved.

### Exceptions / Details
* Do NOT use with finished past time:

    ❌ I have seen him yesterday.

    ✔️ I saw him yesterday.
* Usually prefers unspecified time.

## 4. Present Perfect Continuous 

### Uses + Examples
1) Actions that started in the past and continue now
    * I’ve been studying for three hours.
    * She’s been working here since 2015.
2) Actions with visible results
    * She’s tired because she’s been running.
    * The ground is wet because it’s been raining.
3) Emphasis on duration
    * We’ve been waiting all morning.
    * They’ve been arguing for weeks.

### Exceptions / Details
* Not used with state verbs (know, love, believe).
* Can be used even if the action just stopped, if the result is visible.

## 5. Past Simple 

### Uses + Examples
1) Completed actions
    * I visited my aunt yesterday.
    * She cooked dinner last night.
2) Specific time references
    * We met in 2018.
    * He moved here two months ago.
3) Past habits
    * I played football every weekend.
    * She often walked to school.
4) Actions in a sequence
    * I woke up, made coffee, and left.
    * He entered, sat down, and started reading.

### Exceptions / Details
* Past simple ≠ past perfect.Use past perfect only when ordering two past events.

## 6. Past Continuous 

### Uses + Examples
1) Background actions
    * The birds were singing as I walked.
    * The children were playing outside.
2) Interrupted actions
    * I was studying when the phone rang.
    * She was cooking when the lights went out.
3) Two actions happening simultaneously
    * She was reading while he was driving.
    * They were laughing while I was talking.

### Exceptions / Details
* Not used for short, quick actions:

    ❌ I was dropping my phone.
    
    ✔️️ Past simple instead.

## 7. Past Perfect 

### Uses + Examples
1) Earlier of two past actions
    * She had left when I arrived.
    * The train had departed before we reached the station.
2) Reasons / causes in the past
    * She was sad because she had failed the exam.
    * He felt sick because he had eaten too much.
3) Reported speech (backshifting)
    * “I know her” → He said he had known her.
    * “I lost it” → She said she had lost it.

### Exceptions / Details
* Use only when necessary to clarify order.If order is obvious → past simple is enough.

## 8. Past Perfect Continuous 

### Uses + Examples
1) Duration before a past event
    * They had been waiting for an hour when the bus arrived.
    * I had been studying all night before the exam.
2) Cause of a past state
    * She was tired because she had been working nonstop.
    * He was wet because he had been walking in the rain.

### Exceptions / Details
* Usually focuses on duration or effect.
* Not common with stative verbs.

## 9. Future Simple 

### Uses + Examples
1) Predictions
    * It will rain tomorrow.
    * Prices will rise next year.
2) Promises / offers
    * I’ll help you.
    * I’ll call you later.
3) Instant decisions
    * I’m thirsty — I’ll open the window.
    * “We need bread.” → “I’ll buy some.”
4) Future facts
    * The sun will rise at 6 a.m.
    * The festival will take place in June.

### Exceptions / Details
* For planned future, going to or present continuous is stronger.

## 10. Future Continuous 

### Uses + Examples
1) Actions in progress at a future time
    * At 9 p.m., I’ll be working.
    * Tomorrow at noon, she’ll be traveling.
2) Polite inquiries
    * Will you be using your car tonight?
    * Will you be joining us later?
3) Predicting an ongoing situation
    * He’ll be sleeping at that time.
    * They’ll be studying during the afternoon.

### Exceptions / Details
* Sounds more polite and less direct than “will.”

## 11. Future Perfect 

### Uses + Examples
1) Completed before a future time
    * By Monday, I will have finished the project.
    * By 2030, they will have built the bridge.
2) Deductions about the past (from the future)
    * She will have arrived by now.
    * They will have eaten already.

### Exceptions / Details
* Often paired with by + time expression.
* Not common in spoken English.

## 12. Future Perfect Continuous 

### Uses + Examples
1) Duration before a future time
    * By 2025, I will have been living here for 10 years.
    * By next summer, she will have been studying English for 6 years.
2) Long ongoing actions
    * They will have been working all day.
    * He will have been driving for hours.

### Exceptions / Details
* Used mostly in formal or academic contexts.
* Strong focus on duration.
